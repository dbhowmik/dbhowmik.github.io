PhD Topics

Currently I'm only interested in the following PhD topics. Our lab is equipped with high performance computing facilities including a GPU blade server, high end FPGAs, camera systems (optical and hyperspectral), large data processing infrastructure and access to field equipment (e.g., drone).
  • Neuromorphic Vision System Prompt reaction to an interesting event is a critical feature for any embedded system for safe and reliable interaction with the environment. Computer vision, which plays a significant role in such interaction processes, requires higher level scene understanding with ultra-fast processing capabilities operating at extremely low power. Current vision systems often use compute intensive approaches and are prone to fail in environments with limited power, bandwidth and computing resources, e.g., drones, or edge computing sensors. Processing detailed data from image sensors in real-time is computationally expensive, yet detail is not always necessary. On contrary human visual system has evolved over millions of years to understand the scene around and highly efficient. It using task driven neural processing of (a) shape, texture and photometric properties information; (b) dynamic information. The use of such concepts in hardware-based vision system is largely unexplored. Mimicking the human visual system, this PhD will develop a novel low-power and high-performance neuromorphic vision platform/framework. The platform will be of use to wider applications areas, including robotics, autonomous vehicles, surveillance etc.
  • Generative AI and Remote Sensing Imagery Scene classification and change detection for remote environmental monitoring became significantly important to help in understanding the impact of climate change and taking preventive measures. Satellite imagery is a key component in such scenarios which offers an array of imaging sensors and can provide information in a non-invasive way. However, publicly available satellite data, such as from European Space Agency’s (ESA) Sentinel 1 and 2, are of low resolution, noisy, and not best suitable for remote scene classification. Levarging Generative Artificial Intelligence (AI), this project aims to develop novel algorithms that can create high-resolution maps from low-resolution satellite data. In achieving this goal, the project will research in multimodal image analysis techniques (optical, multi/hyperspectral or synthetic aperture radar (SAR) data captured from satellites and drones). The outcome of this research will be useful to a variety of applications, including but not limited to land cover classification, agricultural and water body monitoring, and disaster management.
  • Media Authenticity in the Age of Artificial Intelligence With the progression of generative AI and deep learning-based techniques, synthetically generated image and deepfake has been commonplace. Deepfakes are getting popular, all for wrong reasons, such as creating fake news and generating fake or manipulative images, videos, and audios for amusement and criminal purposes. It is the need of the hour to develop technologies that can detect deepfakes with high accuracy and stop or minimize the damage caused by them. This PhD proposes development of theories and algorithms that can be used for the media integrity, authenticity and provenance leading to the establishment of trust in media. The proposed project will explore deep learning-based techniques in conjunction with a novel hierarchical feature space exploration approach facilitating trackable content representation. The proposed research will explore various pathways including recent data driven signal decomposition techniques, such as, empirical wavelet transforms in conjunction with statistical parametric estimations and deep learning for unique feature representation. The proposed research constitutes progress towards challenges identified in deepfake detection, e.g., explainable detection, performance benchmarking, temporal aggregation, and physical attribute correlation.
